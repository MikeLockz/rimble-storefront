title: Before you connect modal
description: Grab the code for a ready-made modal template that helps educate users on what they should know before interacting with a blockchain application.
image: "/images/before-you-connect-template.png"
---

The Before you connect modal is designed to educate users and provide some friendly warnings about what connecting means (e.g. the blockchain is public). This might not be necessary if your target user base are very blockchain-savvy.

```js is=react-live hideCode
// import { Heading, Text, Flex, Icon, Modal, MetaMaskButton, Box, Button, Card } from "rimble-ui";

<Card borderRadius={1} p={0}>
  <Box p={4}>
    <Flex justifyContent="space-between">
      <Heading as="h2" my={3}>
        Before you connect
      </Heading>
      <Link>
        <Icon
          name="Close"
          color="moon-gray"
          aria-label="Close and cancel connection"
          pl={4}
        />
      </Link>
    </Flex>
    <Text mb={4}>
      Connecting lets you use [dApp name] via your Ethereum account.
    </Text>
    <Flex flexDirection="row" alignItems="center" mb={3}>
      <Icon
        name="Language"
        size="40px"
        mr={4}
        color="primary"
        display={["none", "block"]}
      />
      <Flex flexDirection="column">
        <Text fontWeight="bold">The blockchain is public</Text>
        <Text>
          Your Ethereum blockchain activity is public. Choose an account you
          don’t mind being linked with your activity here.
        </Text>
      </Flex>
    </Flex>
    <Flex flexDirection="row" alignItems="center" mb={3}>
      <Icon
        name="AccountBalanceWallet"
        size="40px"
        mr={4}
        color="primary"
        display={["none", "block"]}
      />
      <Flex flexDirection="column">
        <Text fontWeight="bold">Have some Ether for fees</Text>
        <Text>
          Using the blockchain requires a fee (like a credit card fee) . Buy
          Ether from exchanges like Coinbase.
        </Text>
      </Flex>
    </Flex>
    <Flex flexDirection="row" alignItems="center">
      <Icon
        name="PeopleOutline"
        size="40px"
        mr={4}
        color="primary"
        display={["none", "block"]}
      />
      <Flex flexDirection="column">
        <Text fontWeight="bold">Have the right account ready</Text>
        <Text>
          If you have multiple Ethereum accounts, check that the one you want to
          use is active in your browser.
        </Text>
      </Flex>
    </Flex>
  </Box>
  <Flex
    flexDirection={["column", "row"]}
    borderTop={1}
    borderColor={"light-gray"}
    justifyContent="flex-end"
  >
    <Box p={4}>
      <Button mb={[2, 0]} width={["100%", "auto"]}>
        Connect
      </Button>
    </Box>
  </Flex>
</Card>
```

</Heading>
<Text
  color="mid-gray"

  The word "Connect" reassures the user they've started the connection process. This area also provides an explanation of what connecting means to new users.
</Text>
<Heading as="h4"
  color="dark-gray">
  2. Things to be aware of
</Heading>
<Text
  color="mid-gray"
  mb={3}
>
  This content should help the user use Ethereum and your dApp safely and successfully. Our example warns the user that their blockchain activity is public, that they'll need some Ether and that they should check which account they're using. But you may want to add to or adapt these points based on what your dApp does. Think of these a bit like terms and conditions.
</Text>

  3. Connect button
</Heading>
<Text
